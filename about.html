Comparison of Window / Shading Performance

Data & Visualizations of EnergyPlus Simulations

Methodology

Simulations were done in EnergyPlus (v 9.5) using the prototype residential buildings provided by National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL). These prototypical files were modified by Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory (LBNL) for the Attachments Energy Rating Council (AERC). The locations included a cold climate (Minneapolis, MN) and a hot climate (Houston, TX).

 

The unshaded insulating glass units (IGUs) where specific for each climate, including window systems (scenarios) that meet the ENERGY STAR® requirements for that location. The windows systems were modeled in LBNL's WINDOW and results exported as BSDF files. Each scenario was included in the EnergyPlus IDF file as a complex glazing system. Simulations were done for unshaded windows, windows with Venetian blinds-between-glass (BBGs) and 1-inch interior Venetian blinds with slat angles between -75 and 75 degrees at increments of 15 degrees, and windows with 3 types of interior cellular blinds (cell-in-cell simulated but not included in this dashboard). Blind slats in WINDOW have a positive angle when tipped up and a negative angle when tipped down roomside.

 

Illumination levels for all simulations were recorded in lux at reference points on the upper level of the south facade, 36 inches from the exterior wall and 30 inches from the floor. Hourly data records the lux level at that hour. Monthly data averages the lux level over the entire month.

Objective of this Dashboard

The EnergyPlus simulation results were compiled using Python/Pandas and then formatted for export. Exported data was used in Tableau to create visualizations specific for this project. The visualizations are intended to look at both the energy and the natural daylighting impacts and to demonstrate the trade-offs on the insulating values of shading devices compared the availability of natural light.
